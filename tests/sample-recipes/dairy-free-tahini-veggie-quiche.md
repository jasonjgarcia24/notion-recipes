**Servings:** 6

## Ingredients
- 6 large eggs
- 3 tablespoons tahini
- 3 tablespoons water
- 1 tablespoon tomato paste
- 1 onion, diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 cups squash, diced small
- 3 cups fresh spinach
- 1 tomato, diced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 0.5 teaspoon salt
- 0.5 teaspoon black pepper
- 0.5 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 3 tablespoons chopped nuts (walnuts or almonds)

## Steps
1. **Preheat oven:** Heat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly oil a 9-inch pie dish or oven-safe skillet.
2. **Cook the aromatics and squash:** Warm 2 tablespoons olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add 1 onion, diced and cook until soft and golden, then add 3 garlic cloves, minced and 2 cups squash, diced small. Cook until the squash is tender, about 8 to 10 minutes.
3. **Build the savory base:** Stir in 1 tablespoon tomato paste and 0.5 teaspoon smoked paprika and cook for another minute until fragrant. Add 3 cups fresh spinach and stir just until it wilts. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.
4. **Make the tahini custard:** In a bowl, whisk 6 large eggs until smooth. In a small cup, stir 3 tablespoons tahini together with 3 tablespoons water until smooth, then add it to the eggs along with 0.5 teaspoon salt and 0.5 teaspoon black pepper.
5. **Combine and assemble:** Spread the cooled vegetables evenly in the pie dish. Pour the egg-tahini mixture over the top, arrange 1 tomato, diced on top, and scatter over 3 tablespoons chopped nuts (walnuts or almonds).
6. **Bake:** Bake for 35 to 45 minutes until the center is set and no longer jiggly and the top is lightly golden. Let it rest 10 minutes before slicing.

## Notes
Tahini replaces the cream here, so whisk it smooth with the water before it
touches the eggs, or it can clump. Keep the squash and spinach from releasing
too much water (don't overcrowd; pat dry if needed) — extra moisture keeps the
custard from setting.
